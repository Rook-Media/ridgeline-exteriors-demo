Vinyl Siding in Naperville, IL

Vinyl siding remains one of the most popular exterior choices in the Chicago suburbs — and for good reason. Modern vinyl products are far superior to the material installed 20 or 30 years ago: fade-resistant, impact-resistant, dimensionally stable, and available in hundreds of color and profile combinations that can match virtually any architectural style.

What Is Modern Vinyl Siding?

Today's vinyl siding products bear little resemblance to the thin, chalky material installed on homes built in the 1970s and 1980s. Leading manufacturers like CertainTeed and Mastic have developed vinyl formulations with UV inhibitors that dramatically slow color fading, impact modifiers that prevent cracking in cold temperatures, and thickness grades that provide meaningful insulating value. The best premium vinyl products carry limited lifetime warranties and are designed to deliver 30–40 years of performance with essentially zero maintenance.

Standard vinyl siding is a hollow profile installed with a built-in nail hem. Insulated vinyl adds a molded foam backing to the profile before installation, which fills the hollow space, improves dimensional stability, and adds meaningful insulating value to the exterior wall assembly. In Chicago's climate, insulated vinyl's thermal performance benefits are real — reducing the temperature differential between the exterior panel and the wall sheathing reduces condensation risk and improves overall energy efficiency.

Vinyl siding is available in dozens of profiles — from traditional 4" double-D lap to Dutch lap, beaded, board and batten, shakes, and custom panels — and in hundreds of factory colors that are built into the material rather than applied as a surface coating. This means the color cannot chip, flake, or peel — ever. The tradeoff is that color change in the future requires either painting (which most manufacturers do not warranty) or replacing the siding entirely.

CertainTeed's Mainstreet and Cedar Impressions lines are popular in our area for their realistic woodgrain texture and excellent color retention. Mastic Home Exteriors produces comparable quality at competitive price points. We work with both manufacturers and can provide samples from each during your estimate so you can evaluate the differences in texture, color range, and profile options in person.

Why Chicago Homeowners Choose Insulated Vinyl

The case for insulated vinyl in Chicago comes down to three factors: thermal performance, dimensional stability, and sound reduction. The continuous foam backing creates a thermal break that reduces heat loss through the wall assembly, which is particularly valuable in older homes where the original insulation may be inadequate or degraded. The same backing eliminates the "drumming" sound that standard hollow vinyl makes in rain and hail — a common complaint from homeowners who upgrade from insulated to standard vinyl unknowingly. And the dimensional stability advantage is real: with foam backing, vinyl panels are less likely to visibly expand and contract with temperature changes, maintaining a flatter, more consistent appearance across the full face of the home.

Our Installation Process

1

Tear-Off & Prep

Existing siding is removed. Substrate is inspected and any damaged sheathing or moisture issues are addressed before proceeding.

2

Moisture Barrier

House wrap is installed over the sheathing, integrated with window and door flashing to create a continuous drainage plane.

3

Vinyl Installation

Siding panels are installed with correct overlap and nail hem attachment, allowing for thermal expansion without buckling.

4

Trim & Accessories

J-channel, F-channel, undersill trim, and corner posts are installed. All penetrations are properly flashed and sealed.

Is Vinyl Right for Your Home?

  • You want a zero-maintenance exterior that never needs painting
  • You're working within a budget that makes fiber cement cost-prohibitive
  • You want a wide selection of colors and profiles to match your home's style
  • You're planning to sell within 10 years and want strong curb appeal at a reasonable cost
  • You want to improve your home's energy efficiency with insulated vinyl

Vinyl Siding FAQ

Modern vinyl siding from premium manufacturers includes UV inhibitors that dramatically reduce fading compared to older products. Some fading over decades is normal, but quality vinyl siding should maintain acceptable color for 20+ years. Lighter colors tend to hold better than darker colors, which absorb more UV energy.

This backing adds thermal insulation, reduces sound transmission, improves dimensional stability, and eliminates the hollow "drumming" sound that standard vinyl produces. The cost premium over standard vinyl is meaningful but the performance benefits are real, particularly in Chicago's climate.

The zero-maintenance aspect (no painting, no sealing) also reduces chemical use over the product's lifetime compared to wood alternatives.
